    Indoors During Earthquake


    • Stay indoors, do not exit building or use elevators.

    • Take cover under table, desk or doorway, if possible.

    • Stay away from all windows and large glass objects.

    • Avoid heavy objects such as lights, wall hangings, etc. which may fall.

    • Help direct people with special needs to a safe place.

    • Wheelchair bound individuals should lock brakes.

    Indoors During Tornado or Thunderstorm


    • Stay indoors, do not exit building or use elevators.

    • Locate an interior room.

    • Go to an eclosed, windowless area in the center of the building (Corners or building support columns are best).

    • Avoid middle of exterior walls.

    • Stay away from all windows and large glass objects.

    • Crouch down and cover your head.

    • Interior stairwells provide shelter and excape.

    • Avoid heavy objects such as lights, wall hangings, etc. which may fall.

    • Remain inside until tornado/storm has passed or cleared to leave.

    • DO NOT use matches or lighter, in case of leaking natural gas or fuel tanks nearby.

    • Help direct people with special needs to a safe place.

    • If instructed to evacuate, (See Evacuation section of Guide).

    Hail Weather Emergency


    • Seek protective shelter immediately.

    • Remain indoors or under protective shelter until hail has stopped, (usually 5 - 10 minutes).

    Lightning Weather Emergency


    • Seek protective shelter immediately.

    • If outdoors, DO NOT stand underneath tall, isolated objects.

    • Avoid projecting above the surrounding landscape.

    • Seek shelter in a low area under a thick growth of small trees or areas such as a ravine or valley.

    • Get off or away from open water as well as metal equipment or small metal vehicles other than automobiles.

    • Stay away from wire fences, clotheslines, metal pipes and rail.

    • If in a group in the open, spread out, keeping people several yards apart.

    • Lightning may strike miles from the parent storm coulds.

    • If you feel your hair stand on end, lightning may be about to strike you.

    • Drop to your knees and bend forward, putting your hands on your knees.

    • DO NOT lie flat on the ground.

    Outdoors During an Earthquake


    • Move to an area away from trees, buildings, walls and power lines.

    • Drop to knees, get into a fetal position.

    • Close your eyes and cross your arms over the back of your neck for protection.

    • Remain in position until shaking has stopped.

    Outdoors During a Tornado


    • Move to an area away from trees, buildings, walls and power lines.

    • Seek lowest possible ground, i.e. ditch, small trench.

    • Stay away from power lines and puddles with wires in them.

    • DO NOT use matches or lighters, in case of leaking gas pipes or fuel tanks.

    • Remain in position until high winds have stopped.

    • DO NOT enter any building that deemed or looks unsafe.

    After Shaking Stops


    • DO NOT use cell phones, except to report serious injuries.

    • Assist in evacuation of people with special needs, if safe.

    • Tune radios to an emergency broadcast or local radio stations for new updates/instructions.

    • Be prepared to evacuate.

    • Police Department will provide instructions for immediate actions by means of door-to-door alerting, police vehicle loud speakers, fire alarms or bull-horns.

    • If instructed to evacuate, (See Evacuation section of Guide).

    • DO NOT enter any building that is deemed/looks unsafe.
