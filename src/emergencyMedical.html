Medical Emergency


  • Call 911 before proceeding with emergency responses.

    • Call 911

    if the Victim is NOT Breathing


    Start rescue breaths:

    • Tilt victim's head back, lift the chin and pinch nose shut.

    • Give 2 short one second breaths (Breathe into victim until chest gently rises).

    • Check for circulation, breathing, coughing and/or movement.

    Next

    if the Victim IS Breathing, and is Choking


    Abdominal Thrusts:

    • DO NOT perform abdominal thrusts if victim is able to speak or cough.

    • Get behind victim, wrap your arms around the persons waist, just above their naval.

    • Clasp your hands together in a double fist. Press in and up in QUICK thrusts.

    • Repeat procedure until choking stops.

    • Report incident to supervisor. Supervisor is responsible for notifying ASU EH&S.

    If there are Signs of circulation

    Do the following:

    • Give one slow breath every 5 seconds.

    • Recheck for circulation and breathing every minute.

    • Continue rescue breaths until victim is breathing or medical assistance arrives.

    If there is No Sign of Circulation

    If AED available and trained person present or can be assisted by 911 operator:

    Find AED Locations:

    AED Locations
    • DO NOT use the AED on patient laying in water or on any conductive surface.

    • DO NOT use AED in potentially explosive environment.

    • DO NOT use AED if victim is less than 1 year old.

    • Proceed with using AED unit.

    • AED unit is automated and will only shock when necessary.

    • If AED is used, please contact ASU Environmental Health & Safety (See Emergency Contacts).

    OR

    Start Cardio Pulmonary Resuscitation (CPR):

    • If victim is an adult: Find the notch where lower ribs meet breastbone. Place heel of hand on breastbone. Place other hand on top of the first.

    • If victim is aged 1-8: Use only one hand.

    • If victim is a baby: Check for airway obstruction first. Imagine a horizontal line drawn between the baby's nipples. Place two fingers of one hand just below this line, in the center of the chest.

    • Position shoulders over your hands. Compress chest 30 times (1-1/2 to 2 inches) using your upper body weight (unless baby) and a smooth, even rhythm.

    • Ensure that airway is open, and give 2 short 1 second rescue breaths.

    • Do five (5) more sets of (30) compressions and (2) rescue breaths.

    • Check for signs of circulation.

    • Call 911 if you haven't yet. Continue CPR until signs of life are detected, or help arrives.

    If there ARE Signs of Circulation


    If sign of circulation but NOT breathing:

    • Give one slow breath every 5 seconds. Until victim responds or medical assistance arrives.


    If NO circulation, NOT breathing:

    • Contiue CPR until medical assistance arrives.
