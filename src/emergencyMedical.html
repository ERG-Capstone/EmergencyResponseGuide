    Victim NOT Breathing


    Start rescue breaths:

    • Tilt victim's head back, lift the chin and pinch nose shut.

    • Give 2 short one second breaths (Breathe into victim until chest gently rises).

    • Check for circulation, breathing, coughing and/or movement.

    Signs of circulation, NOT breathing


    Do the following:

    • Give one slow breath every 5 seconds.

    • Recheck for circulation and breathing every minute.

    • Continue resue breaths until victim is breathing or medical assistance arrives.

    No Sign of Circulation, NOT Breathing


    If AED available and trained person present or can be assisted by 911 operator:

    Find AED Locations:

    AED Locations
    • Proceed with using AED unit.

    • DO NOT use the AED on patient laying in water or on any conductive surface.

    • DO NOT use AED in potetially explosive environment.

    • AED unit is automated and will only shock when necessary.

    • If AED is used, please contact ASU Environmental Health & Safety (See Emergency Contacts).

    OR

    Start Cardio Pulmonary Resuscitation (CPR):

    • Find the notch where lower ribs meet breastbone. Place heel of hand on breastbone. Place other hand on top of the first.

    • Position shoulders over your hands. Compress chest 30 times (1-1/2 to 2 inches) using a smooth, even rhythm.

    • Give 2 short 1 second resue breaths.

    • Do five (5) more sets of (30) compressions and (2) rescue breaths.

    • Check for signs of circulation.

    There ARE Signs of Circulation


    If sign of circulation but NOT breathing:

    • Give one slow breath every 5 seconds. Until victim responds or medical assistance arrives.


    If NO circulation, NOT breathing:

    • Contiue CPR until medical assistance arrives.
