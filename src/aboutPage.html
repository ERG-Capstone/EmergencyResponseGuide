Emergency Preparedness at ASU


  • Please read this guide before an emergency occurs!

  • Emergencies, disasters, accidents, injuries and crimes can occur at any time

  • Being physically and psychologically prepared is an individual/organizational responsibility

  • Acquaint yourself with the contents within this guide. Preparedness is often defined by how well your respond to and recover from an incident.

  • Preload important phone numbers listed on the Contacts page into your mobile phone.

  • BE PREPARED!

Preparedness Checklist

Keep emergency supplies in your office


Supplies to consider:

  • Medications

  • Flashlights

  • Comfortable Shoes

  • Bottled Water

  • Batteries

  • Portable Batteries

Post an Emergency Response Guide in a visible location in your office. Ensure all staff are made aware of its location.

Become familiar with all exit routes and assembly points around your building.

Locate the nearest fire extinguisher and pull station.

Complete and remain current on annual fire safety training.

Register for Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR), Automated External Defibrillator (AED), First Aid, Crime Prevention, or other safety training courses.

Emergency Recovery at ASU


  • After an emergency occurs, RECOVERY is the next step.

  • ASU is insured for losses to University-owned property, as well as injury to non-employees and employees on-the-job.

  • A claim must be generated in order to make the loss known to appropriate ASU representatives, and ASU's self-insurance provider.

Property and Liability Losses

  • Property claims that exceed $10,000 must be reported within 24-hours.

  • Property claims of less than $10,000 must be reported within 90 days.

  • A death or serious injury must be reported within 24-hours to Insurance Services.

  • Information on filing and various claim forms are available on the Insurance Website.

Workers' Comp Losses

  • Workers' compensation claims must be reported within 48-hours (See Contacts section).

  • Questions regarding workers' compensation should be directed to Human Resources (See Contacts section).

  • Information and forms for filing workers' compensation claims are available at HR Forms Website.

Follow up on potential/sustained injuries

  • Report accidents and incidents involving injury to your supervisor and to the Environmental Health & Safety Loss Prevention Coordinator. (See Contacts section)

  • Information and forms for filing an accident and incident report are available at EH&S Forms Website.
