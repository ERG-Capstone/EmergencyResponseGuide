Hazardous Materials Incident

General

  • A hazardous materials incident is considered a spill of any of the following: Chemicals (liquid and solids), Hazardous waste, or Oils (fuel oil: diesel and gasoline).

  • Only Trained and Authorized Personnel Are Permitted to Respond to Hazardous Materials Incident.

  • Employees must be trained in the safe use of chemicals and chemical waste management prior to working in a laboratory or cleaning up minor spills.

  • A minor spill is one trained employees are capable of containing safely without assistance from EH&S or emergency personnel.

  • All other spills considered major. For additional guidance on chemical safety, refer to the Hazardous Waste Management Compliance Guide.

Major Spill/Leak

  • If conditions warrant, activate nearest fire alarm.

  • Immediately evacuate the area, closing doors behind you. Refer to the Evacuation section of this guide.

  • Call 911.

  • DO NOT attempt to clean up the spill yourself.

  • Provide Safety Data Sheets and other pertinent information to response personnel.

Minor Spill/Leak

  • Do not attempt to clean-up even a minor spill unless you feel comfortable doing so.

  • NEVER attempt to clean-up a spill if you are alone.

  • Alert people in the immediate area.

  • Wear proper protective equipment before attempting to clean up.

  • Avoid breathing vapors and confine spill to a small area.

  • DO NOT ALLOW ANYTHING TO DISCHARGE INTO DRAINS!

  • Use your spill kit.

  • Bag cleanup material and label with Hazardous Waste Label.

  • Contact EH&S for waste removal at the number listed in the attached contacts page Monday-Friday 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. (leave a detailed message after hours).

Chemical Spill on Body

  • Register for Training: Laboratory Chemical Safety or Hazardous Waste Management Techniques Environmental Health & Safety website.

  • Flood exposed area with running water for at least 15 minutes.

  • Avoid contact with eyes.

  • Remove any contaminated clothing, place into plastic bag and seal.

  • Ensure no contamination in shoes or under jewelry.

  • Check Material Safety Data Sheets to determine if delayed effects may be expected.

  • Obtain medical attention at the nearest ASU Health Services, urgent care or hospital. For locations nearest you, contact ASU Health Services using the number listed in the contacts page.

  • Report all incidents to a supervisor. The supervisor is responsible for workers compensation paperwork.

Respond to Incident

  • Register for Training: Laboratory Chemical Safety or Hazardous Waste Management Techniques Environmental Health & Safety website.

  • REPORT INCIDENT to supervisor. Supervisor is responsible to notify ASU Environmental Health & Safety (EH&S).

  • Contact ASU Environmental Health & Safety (See Emergency Contacts section) which has personnel trained in the proper clean-up and containment of minor hazardous spills or releases.
